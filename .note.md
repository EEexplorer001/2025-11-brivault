### [H-1] Attacker can aggregate other losers' shares to drain vault's assets in a share-stuffing attack.
### [H-2] Loser can redeem assets after winner is set. 
### [H-3] No increment staking in `BriVault::deposit`, causing players who deposit multiple times and want to cancel participation lose funds.
### [H-4] Attacker can start a griefing attack by calling `BriVault::joinEvent` for every country to dilute winner's prize.
### [H-5] No reset for `BriVault::userSharesToCountry` in `BriVault::cancelParticipation` will dilute winner's prize if an attacker deposits a large amount and then cancels participation.

### [M-1] No check for duplicate or invalid countries in `BriVault::setCountry`, causing prize claim confliction. Some winners might not be able to withdraw prize.
### [M-2] No duplicate players check and upper bound limit for number of players. Gas-DoS attack is possible via calling `BriVault::joinEvent` many times.
### [M-3] Owner has too much centuality: owner can set winners all by themselves.
### [M-4] Owner has too much centuality: owner can call `BriVault::setCountry` at any time to overwrite previous countries. Winner's prize might get locked.
### [M-5] Owner has too much centuality: no check for time sequential constraints in initialization. 
### [M-6] No check for 0 address. 
### [M-7] Player can deposit on behalf of receiver. Receiver can join without any deposit and call `BriVault::cancelParticipation` and get player's deposit.
